Problem Statement: Ubearable Gym Wait Times

The increased interest in the gym on campus has caused the wait times to utilize gym equipment to grow exponentially, and it has become overcrowded.

Affinity diagram: Brainstrom Breakdown of The Gym Queue App

Our affinity diagram tackled 5 main subjects of thought. We had our ideas on how we would start up our company/technology, the ideas revolving around how the product worked and where we would go with the project; whether that was taking it to a more physical side on the gym machines themselves or sticking witht the idea of an app and using nfc tags or QR codes. We also brainstormed about partnerships either with gym equipment companies, gym chains like Golds or Planet Fitness, and what partnerships with colleges would look like and how they would benefit us. Lastly our final two branches of thought were any open questions we had about our products mainly focusing on the buisness aspect and advertisment and how we would go about approaching that.

Team Personas: Users From Around the Country]

My persona was David Smith, a 25 year oldmale from Miami Florida who struggles with time management with his busy schedule. This affects his ability to work out at the gym effectively bringing him to our app. He stated that the issue was that "My busy day to day, makes working our difficult with equipment wait times" He is luckily good with mobile apps making this app very easy to use for him as long as we tailor the app to be easy for users, and it targets his specific needs as well almost perfectly.

Team Storyboards: Examples of App Application

My story board is fairly simple. It emcaptulates David Smiths gym routine and how it would be changed and what it would look like with the usage of our app. It shows how easy it is for David to plan out his gym routine ahead of time to minimize the amount of time in the gym and maximize the amount of time working out. This example only focuses on the squat rack but could be used for realistically almost any machine or workout area.

Team Sketches: Brainstorming Protoype App Ideas

Our sketches are pretty simple, they are all very similar. They are illustrating possible ways our app could look to users. As well as how it would work. What buttons would be pressed, what would you see when entering a queue, and when you want to leave as well.

Team Paper Prototype: The Gym Queue and Tracker App Paper Preview

Our paper prototype focused on a lot of the important aspects that one would wonder about a new app. For example, making and loging into an account. The purpose of the app, which is seeing which machines are available/how long the wait is for another machine or how many people are infront of you. The video also talks about how the app can be tailored to your specific wants and needs by specifying how many reps, sets, and the weight that you are planning to do. This is also useful for tracking things like macros and calories burnt, and rest time because it gets into the speficics of what you are doing intsead of just tracking your movement, this would be the workout tracker. The app also shows you weight time and allows you to join and leave a queue once joined. Not only that the settings allow you to change things to become even more personalized for you, for example there is an option to have your rest and workout data tracked so the app can find out the best possible rest time for you. Finally the video also show cases a Crowd Meter button that lets gym goers know how busy a gym might be, whether or not they'll have trouble getting around and using machines based on wait times ecetera.
